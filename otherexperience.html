Teaching/Work Experience

Supplemental Instruction Leader, SI/EXCEL Program

I communicate with the professor to develop helpful review sessions and handouts for a traditionally difficult course. I also lead review sessions to elucidate and communicate course material with an emphasis on stimulating peer collaboration to promote student learning.

Leadership Experience

Student Supervisor, SI/EXCEL Program

Since May 2019, I have been one of 7 Student Supervisors in the SI/EXCEL Program. I oversee and observe other 70 leaders’ sessions and offer constructive feedback to yield more effective communication of the material and collaboration in sessions to maximize student learning across 28 different traditionally difficult courses. I also communicate with the SI/EXCEL administrators, acting as a bridge between the whole program and the administration. In addition, I mentor leaders by helping to troubleshoot issues they may be experiencing in their sessions, and facilitate their ongoing training and development as Leaders during monthly meetings.

Co-President, MCS SAC

Since April 2019, I have been the Co-President for the Mellon College of Science Student Advisory Council (MCS SAC) at Carnegie Mellon University. I communicate and organize information discussed during meetings and results thereof with over 20 student and 5 faculty SAC members. I help to advance goals in all committees including fundraising, service, and events. I lead the organization of two Mellon College of Science events aimed toward fostering community within the college. I also served as Secretary from April 2018 – April 2019, and as a member since October 2017.

Board Member, University Disciplinary & Academic Review Boards

Since January 2019, I have been a member of the University Disciplinary and Academic Review Boards at Carnegie Mellon. These are committees of students and faculty that conduct hearings for students that break the Carnegie Mellon code of conduct. After the hearing, board members make a recommendation regarding what disciplinary action the university should pursue.
